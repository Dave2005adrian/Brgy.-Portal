About Our Barangay

Barangay Mulapula stands as a vital pillar of Passi City’s agricultural landscape, balancing its deep historical roots with modern advancements in sustainability and community development. As a key participant in the city’s identity as the "Sweet City," the barangay serves as a primary hub for the cultivation of sugarcane, rice, and pineapples. In recent years, Mulapula has embraced provincial initiatives such as the MoRProGRes Gardens program, which integrates ornamental gardening with food security, encouraging residents to maintain communal plots that bolster both local nutrition and the area's natural aesthetic. These efforts are supported by the Department of Agriculture’s modernization programs, providing local farmers with "Tekno Kliniks" and mechanized equipment to ensure climate-resilient crop production. The physical landscape of the barangay is anchored by essential community landmarks that facilitate both governance and social cohesion. The Barangay Hall and its adjacent multi-purpose pavement serve as the central venue for health missions, agricultural aid distribution, and the vibrant celebrations of the annual Barangay Fiesta. Education remains a cornerstone of the community at Mulapula Elementary School, which provides the foundational learning for the village’s youth. Furthermore, the spiritual life of the residents is reflected in the well-maintained local chapels, which stand as symbols of the enduring faith and cultural traditions that have characterized the region since the Spanish colonial era. Infrastructure developments in 2026 have further integrated Mulapula into the broader economic engine of Passi City. Enhanced Farm-to-Market Roads (FMR), supported by the Philippine Rural Development Project, have significantly reduced transportation costs for local growers, allowing fresh produce to reach urban centers and the nearby Triple "A" accredited slaughterhouse in Barangay Salngan more efficiently. Combined with the expansion of reliable utilities through ILECO II and modernized water systems, Mulapula continues to evolve into a progressive rural community. This growth honors its history as a wartime sanctuary and ancient settlement while positioning itself as a model for sustainable agricultural living in Central Panay.
